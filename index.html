About

Unpacking the mechanics of consequential choice in a variety of settings. Individuals, algorithms, and institutions decide under uncertainty, and what happens when those systems fail.

This work draws on game theory, behavioral economics, complexity science, and computer science. I'm a Research Fellow at Harvard's Belfer Center, where I run Move37, a project developing AI tools for negotiation and conflict management. I also advise investors and technology companies on strategy and decision architecture.

I completed my master's at Harvard Kennedy School, where my thesis examined AI inference optimization. My background as a professional athlete informs how I think about performance, decisions, and executing under pressure.
